Introduction

For some time I've been the owner of the Thrustmaster Warthog HOTAS Flightstick and Throttle. It's a great combo, but very soon it was evident that I should get some kind of mount to make full use of it.

Searching through the internet I found that some use VESA brackets for mounting their HOTAS directly onto the chair. Given the weight and especially the size of the Warthog throttle I wasn't really sure whether this would work for the Thrustmasters. After digging a little bit deeper I found the Monstertech Table Mounts and took a closer look at these.

The monetary side

Given the price tag I've been a little hesitant at first. The shop gives a price of 178.00 € for the silver Warthog flight stick and throttle mounts, as well as a whopping 218.00 € for the black ones. However, this is still without delivery costs and excluding (!) taxes. A EU customer who's being charged 19% VAT will therefore pay in the end about 222.65 € for the silver mounts and approximately 270.25 € for the black ones, depending on shipping costs.

Since the Warthog flight stick and throttle could be purchased on Amazon for a total of 333.78 €, making them just a little more expensive than the Monstertech mounts, I took some time to find possible alternatives, but decided in the end that I would give them a try.

The package

Three weeks after placing my order, I unpackaged the following:

The table mounts as they're delivered, without mounting plates.
The mounting plates for the flight stick and the throttle.
The position of the mounting point is adjustable. The mount itself is attached to the table with a clamp.

Assembling the mounts

Using my own 5mm and 6mm hex keys and the little instruction manual which comes with the package, I was able to assemble the mounts in about one hour. While not too difficult, the weight of the items is noticeable. In the end, the assembled throttle and mount have a combined weight of more than 6kg.

Attaching the flight stick plate onto the mount.
The Warthog flight stick on the Monstertech Mount and attached to the table.
The throttle attached onto the mounting plate. Combined weight of throttle and plate is at about 6,3 kg.

The Monstertech mounts in action

For all the money you're surely getting really, really solid mounts. Once the screws are tight, I'd rather worry about the table than the mounts or the Thrustmasters.
